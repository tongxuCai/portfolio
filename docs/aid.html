China's government financial investments expand overseas fast, big and unnoticed.

China is undoubtedly occupying the headlines of all major headlines today. However, it is still beyond the common knowledge about the scale of China's international financial deployement. In this project, the readers could learn a detailed breakdown of these projects time and geographic wise.

By Frida Cai

Published August 8, 2019

Chinese government does not participate in any public recording of its global financial activities. So far, the dataset published by AidData in 2018 is the most comprehensive source of Chinese official projects overseas.

The projects are categorized under Official Development Assistance (ODA, commonly known as Aid), Other Official Flows (OOF that is primarily for commercial or representative intentions) and Vague Official Finance (Vague OF).

Chinese overseas financial activities in 2000 and 2014

Sources: https://www.aiddata.org/data/geocoded-chinese-global-official-finance-dataset

The following chart describes how financial flows, goods or services are transferred from the donor to recipient for a project. Flow types include Debt Forgiveness, Debt Rescheduling, Export Credits, Foreign Direct Investment, Freestanding Technical Assistance, Grant, Loan, Joint Venture with Recipient, Scholarships/Training in Donor Country, and Strategic/Supplier Credit.
